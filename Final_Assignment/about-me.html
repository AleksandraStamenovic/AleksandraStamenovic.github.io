About Me

Karate is not just a martial art; it’s a journey of discipline, strength, and perseverance. For those who have dedicated years to mastering its techniques, as I dedicated 9 of mine, the black belt is a symbol of their hard work and commitment. Among these dedicated practitioners, women have made significant strides, breaking barriers and setting new standards. Here I would like to celebrate some of women who inspired me and many other girls to join and to work hard to achieve my karate goals.

Rika Usami: The Queen of Kata

Rika Usami from Japan is a name synonymous with excellence in karate. Known for her powerful and precise Kata performances, Usami has captivated audiences worldwide. Her journey in karate began at a young age, and her dedication led her to become a World Karate Champion. Usami’s performances are a blend of grace and strength, showcasing her deep understanding of the art. Her ability to execute complex movements with such precision makes her a true inspiration for karatekas everywhere.

Valeria Kumizaki: The Brazilian Powerhouse

Valeria Kumizaki from Brazil is a force to be reckoned with in the Kumite category. A Pan American Games gold medalist, Kumizaki’s achievements in karate are a result of her relentless training and fighting spirit. Her ability to strategize and execute techniques with precision has earned her numerous accolades. Kumizaki’s journey is an inspiration to many, demonstrating that with determination and hard work, one can achieve greatness in the martial arts.

Irina Zaretska: The Azerbaijani Champion

Irina Zaretska from Azerbaijan is another outstanding athlete in the Kumite category. She has made a significant impact on the international karate scene, winning numerous medals at World and European Championships. Zaretska’s fighting style is both aggressive and strategic, allowing her to dominate her opponents. Her dedication to the sport and her impressive achievements make her a unique role model.
